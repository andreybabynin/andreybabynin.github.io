Famous ML failures - presentation at HSE (November'23)

On a brisk November day, I found myself at the masters programmes demo-day at HSE's Faculty of Computer Science, immersing in the atmosphere of my alma mater. My presentation, titled "ML fuckups: an overview of famous failures", was set before an audience of current and prospective students.

Drawing inspiration from Tolstoy's wisdom, I began with a remark:

"All happy families are alike; each unhappy family is unhappy in its own way."

Just as with families, in the world of ML, successes may be similar, but each failure is unique. The main goal of my talk was to draw parallels between projects in ML and other fields, and to remind everyone of Murphy's Law, which states: if something can go wrong, it most certainly will.

The presentation also included a reference to Nolan's portrayal of Oppenheimer, highlighting the irony that success can be as dangerous as failure.
